In the podcast, Balaji talks about an axis with technological progressives on one side, and political progressives on the other side.

When faced with a problem, political progressives look at politics first for solutions. New laws or subsidies. In contrast, technological progressives look at technology first for solutions.

Both groups tend to agree on the problems. For instance, road fatalities, COVID-19, and climate change are all problems. But they disagree on the mechanism of change.

Balaji argues that we should bet more on technology to solve certain problems. Technology is a voluntary vehicle for change.

Technological improvements are the thing to bet on. Technology has consistently improved over the last hundred plus years. This type of progress, Balaji argues, is more certain than political progress.

In this light, Balaji introduces the concept of an axis with technological progressives on one side, and political progressives or technological conservatives on the other side.
